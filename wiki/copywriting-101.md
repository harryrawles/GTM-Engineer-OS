# Copywriting 101

Core principles for signal-based cold email. Read this before writing any copy.

---

## Hard Rules

- **Offer is king** - biggest lever on performance, always test this first
- **Word count (canonical - referenced by all other files):**
  - ATL (VP / C-Level / Director): **max 60 words**
  - BTL (Manager / IC): **max 90 words**
  - Default cold: **75 words target, 100 hard ceiling**
  - Every word earns its place
- **Subject lines: 2-4 words** - curiosity-led, not descriptive
- **No vanity personalisation** - "saw you hired 3 SDRs" not "I admire your passion for revolutionising the industry"
- **Split test ONE variable at a time** - scientific method only (see wiki/scientific-method.md)
- **Each follow-up tests a distinct cognitive mechanism** - not just different wording
- **Very low friction CTAs** - prospect should be able to reply in 5 words or less
- **Every single word must earn its place** - cut anything that doesn't move the reader forward
- **Passes the bar test** - could you read this out loud to someone at a bar?
- **PRR is the north star metric** - positive reply rate, not open rate
- **No question marks except the CTA** - delete every other question mark
- **2:1 ratio** - more about them than about you
- **Pattern breaks** - look different to every other cold email they receive
- **Spintax and liquid syntax** - vary copy at scale to avoid spam filters. See `gtm-skills/spintax-ninja.md` for the exact format and process.
- **No spam words in the body either** - same trigger list as `wiki/subject-lines.md` Hard Rules ("free", "guarantee", "limited time", "act now"), bend only when the client's own offer genuinely requires the word

## Choosing a Campaign Style

Pick the campaign's overall angle before drafting individual emails - this decision shapes every email in
the sequence, not just the first one.

**Offer-Led.** Build a valuable offer tied into the client's core product. Ask: would someone actually pay
for this? Does it feel like it was built specifically for them? Is saying no almost unreasonable? See
`wiki/offer-creation-theory.md` and `wiki/offer-framework.md` for how to build the offer itself.

**Pro-Bono Play.** Give something away for free as the hook. See `best-practices/playbooks/ai-calling.md`
for a worked example (a pro-bono AI calling agent offer for dealerships) before writing one from scratch.

**Three Ideas / Creative Angle.** Lead with insight or a framework rather than an offer. Works well for
consultative or strategic clients. This is the strategic version of the "Information Arbitrage" and "Dream
Client Identification" tactics under Winning Strategies below.

**Exception - the client already has a strong offer.** A VC looking for acquisition targets, a podcast
seeking speakers, an agency offering free leads: lead with that directly rather than forcing one of the
three styles above onto an offer that's already sharp.

## Bias Application

Every piece of copy can leverage cognitive biases. The structured mapping lives in `wiki/psychological-principles.md` - see the "Biases by Copy Stage" table. See "The Formula" below for the per-part breakdown (2-3 biases per part) and `gtm-skills/cold-email-writer.md`'s "Overdone-It bias" rule for the total ceiling (12-15 biases across the whole email, minimum 6) - overloading triggers Overdone-It bias, copy reads as manipulative and rejection rises.

## The Complete Chain

Every email must trigger this sequence in order. If any step fails, the chain breaks.

| Step | Action | Stimulus required |
|------|--------|------------------|
| 1 | See subject in inbox | Curiosity / relevance - not deleted |
| 2 | Read preview text | Pattern break - not categorised as spam |
| 3 | Open email | Value promise confirmed |
| 4 | Read body copy | Feel understood |
| 5 | Process offer | See clear path to goal |
| 6 | Build confidence | Trust you can help |
| 7 | Take action | Reply / click / book |

### Corresponding Stimuli

| Element | Goal | Benchmark |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Subject line | Gets open | 15-50% open rate |
| Preview text | Reinforces open decision | - |
| Opening line | Gets read (pattern break) | - |
| Body copy | Creates understanding + pain awareness | - |
| Offer positioning | Builds confidence | - |
| CTA | Elicits clear action | - |
| Follow-up sequence | Persistence bias | - |

## The Formula

Every email follows this structure. No exceptions.

For each part, deliberately leverage 2-3 biases from `wiki/psychological-principles.md` and avoid triggering 1-2.

**Part 1 - Personalisation (Why you, why now)**
One sentence proving you looked at them. Must NOT signal a pitch is coming.
- "Saw you just hired 3 SDRs" - good
- "I admire your passion for revolutionising the industry" - delete

*Biases to leverage:* Subjective validation (their exact problem), Relatability, Understanding bias, Familiarity (in follow-ups). *Avoid triggering:* Egocentric bias, Excessive self-regard (do not compliment vaguely).

**Part 2 - Who Am I (Social proof first, then what you do)**
"I run outbound for 3 SaaS teams, one just hit $200K pipeline last month."
- Use "I" not "we"
- Specific numbers, named logos where possible

*Biases to leverage:* Authority misinfluence, Social proof, Show-and-tell, Quantitative specificity bias, Conviction, Casual bias. *Avoid triggering:* Excessive self-regard (do not oversell yourself), Click-whirr conditioning.

**Part 3 - The Offer (Most important part)**
Frame as giving, not selling. Use "X in Y or Z" where possible: specific outcome, timeframe, guarantee.
Lead with a give-first element: audit, teardown, sample, insight.
- Observation about their situation + outcome + risk reversal
- Make saying no feel irrational

*Biases to leverage:* Reward super-response (the incentive), Deprival super-reaction (loss aversion if they do not act), Punishment super-response addressed via risk reversal, Anchoring (number before pricing), Contrast misreaction, Justification (because…), Disqualified identity (who this is NOT for), One-of-a-kind, Removed self-concerns, Hyperbolic discounting (immediate win), Tried-and-tested OR Novelty (pick one - not both). *Avoid triggering:* Ambiguity effect, Cognitive dissonance head-on.

**Part 4 - CTA (One binary yes/no question)**
Prospect should be able to reply in 5 words or less.
- "Want me to send it over?" - good
- "Feel free to reach out at your earliest convenience" - delete

**Never lead with a meeting ask on Email 1.** Asking for a call before any value has landed is the highest-
friction CTA available, save it for later in the sequence once the offer has proven itself. Use a
low-resistance ask instead:
- "Is this relevant to {{company}}?"
- "Could I record a short video for you?"
- "Could I send some more info?"

The third option feeds directly into `gtm-skills/reply-handler.md` STEP 2F (Stall - "send more info"), which
already has the decision tree for what happens when a prospect takes that option.

*Biases to leverage:* Action bias, Reason-respecting tendency ("because…"), Permission-based mis-influence ("mind if…"), Lil' bonus, Instruction clarity, Curiosity tendency. *Avoid triggering:* Ambiguity effect, Cognitive closure (do not close the loop for them).

## Frameworks

### T.I.P.S

**Trigger.** Why are you reaching out right now? New hire, team expansion, funding, product launch. Make it explicit in line one.

**Implication.** What does that trigger likely mean for them? Show you joined the dots, not just sprayed.

**Pain.** What problem does your ICP usually face in that situation? Show the cost of doing nothing.

**Social Proof.** Name a real client, name a real outcome. Ties directly into your solution.

**Solution.** Briefly explain how you got that outcome. Don't oversell - the goal is a reply, not a closed deal.

**Soft CTA.** "Worth a chat?" "Open to hearing more?" Low friction. Gets a reply, not a commitment.

**TIPS Example:**

> Hey Sam,
>
> Looks like you're hiring AEs in DC. Imagine ramping them is a priority.
>
> Most sales leaders struggle to get reps ramped under 5 months, meaning hitting quarterly targets becomes an uphill battle.
>
> We helped Gong's reps ramp in under 3 months with our coaching programme.
>
> Open to seeing how?

---

### The 5 C's

**Clear.** What do you actually do? Strip the jargon. If they have to re-read your first line, you've lost them.

**Concise.** Every word should earn its place. Cut anything that doesn't move the reader forward.

**Compelling.** Speak to what they actually care about - goals, fears, frustrations. Make them feel seen before making them an offer.

**Credible.** Claims without proof are noise. One real number, one real client name beats ten vague promises.

**Call to Action.** One action, one ask. Vague endings get vague responses.

## Sequence Structure

| Email | Day | Thread | Purpose |
|-------|-----|--------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Net new | Best angle, strongest offer, full pitch |
| 2 | Day 3 | Threaded to #1 | Different value prop, shorter, add what you cut from Email 1 |
| 3 | Day 7 | Net new thread | Completely different angle, lower friction CTA |
| 4 | Optional | Threaded to #3 | Hail mary: "is this even your responsibility?" |

Never just bump. Every follow-up takes a different angle and tests a different cognitive mechanism.

---

## The 3 Value Props - Rotate Across Emails

Every B2B offer does one of three things. Lead with one per email. Never repeat the same one.

| Value prop | What it targets |
|-----------|----------------|
| Make money | More pipeline, more revenue |
| Save time | Automation, efficiency, removing manual work |
| Save money | Cut costs, reduce headcount, kill bad contracts |

---

## Chunking Up vs Down

When an offer isn't landing, reframe it.

**Chunk up** - broader outcomes, zero features. Pipeline growth, revenue impact, scale without headcount.

**Chunk down** - the small annoying jobs they don't want to think about. Deliverability management, data sourcing, copywriting methodology, no long-term contracts.

Test both. Find what resonates. Offer is still king.

## Decision Maker Pain Points

Match copy angle to who you're targeting.

| Role | Measured on | Copy angle |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| VP Sales | Quota attainment, conversion rates, ramp time | Revenue outcomes, specific numbers, what good looks like vs where they are |
| CTO | Integration complexity, technical debt, security | Technical credibility, what breaks at scale, precision over urgency |
| CFO | Cost efficiency, ROI, financial risk | Hard numbers, payback period, cost of inaction - never soft benefits |
| Head of Ops | Process efficiency, cross-functional coordination | Time saved, process simplified, fewer tools not more |

## Winning Strategies

**Case Study Match.** Link proof to a similar company. $143,503 beats $140K every time. Use the real number.

**Dream Client Identification.** Signal that you hold the keys to connecting them with their ideal customer.

**Problem Sniffing.** Find an actual problem - bad reviews, a gap vs competitors - and reference it directly.

**Asset-Based Outreach.** Lead with something you made for them. The pitch becomes "here's something free" not "buy my thing."

**Information Arbitrage.** Share intel they don't have. Positions you as value-first before asking for anything.

**Implication Over Personalisation.** Sometimes zero variables outperforms heavy personalisation. Vague-but-resonant lets the prospect fill in the blanks.
