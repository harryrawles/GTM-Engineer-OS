# ATL vs BTL Messaging

Above-the-Line (executives) and Below-the-Line (managers / ICs) need totally different copy. Using ATL language with an IC fails because it is too abstract. Using BTL language with a VP fails because they delegate it. Choose the right register before writing.

---

## Quick Decision Tree

```
Decision-maker title?
├─ VP / C-Level / Director / Head of → ATL
└─ Manager / Specialist / Analyst / Coordinator / IC → BTL
```

Multi-thread (both) when:
- Complex enterprise sale
- Multiple stakeholders involved
- Both budget holder and end-user buy-in needed

## ATL — Above-the-Line

**Target:** VP, C-Level, Director, Head of.

**How they think:** Past and future. Strategic. They want validation, not problems solved. They care about the big picture, not daily operations.

**What they care about:**
- Revenue impact
- Risk reduction
- Strategic initiatives
- Competitive positioning
- Board / investor optics

**Message length:** 2-3 sentences. Maximum.

### Language to use

- "Strategic initiative"
- "Revenue impact"
- "Competitive advantage"
- "Board-level priority"
- "Risk mitigation"
- "What would it mean for {{Q2 / annual / board}} targets if..."

### Rules

- 2-3 sentences only. Executives skim.
- Lead with outcome, not process. They care about WHAT changes, not HOW it works.
- No operational details. Mentioning daily workflows gets you delegated to a subordinate.
- Question format works well — prompt strategic reflection.

### Fatal ATL mistake

Talking about operational problems (hours saved, manual processes, workflow fixes) to an executive = instant delegation. The email gets forwarded down and you lose the thread.

### When to target ATL

- Deal size over $50K ACV
- Strategic / transformation purchase
- Requires budget approval from the top
- Long sales cycle is acceptable

### ATL Examples

**CEO of mid-market SaaS**
```
Subject: revenue efficiency

{{firstName}},

If you could identify revenue leakage 3 months faster, what would that mean for next year's targets?

We helped {{similar_company}}'s CEO do exactly that.

15 minutes to explore?
```

**VP Sales at a scaling company**
```
Subject: pipeline visibility

{{firstName}},

{{company}}'s growth from {{X}} to {{Y}} ARR usually exposes blind spots in pipeline forecasting.

{{similar_company}}'s VP Sales closed that gap and beat board targets by 18% last quarter.

Worth a conversation?
```

**CTO evaluating security**
```
Subject: risk exposure

{{firstName}},

The average cost of a data breach in {{industry}} hit $4.5M this year.

{{similar_company}}'s CTO reduced their attack surface by 60% in 90 days.

Open to a quick comparison?
```

## BTL — Below-the-Line

**Target:** Managers, Specialists, Analysts, Coordinators, Individual Contributors.

**How they think:** Present. Tactical. They want problems solved immediately. They care about daily workflow, not strategic vision.

**What they care about:**
- Daily pain points
- Time savings
- Workflow improvements
- Making their job easier
- Looking good to their boss

**Message length:** 3-4 sentences acceptable.

### Language to use

- "Stop spending 3 hours every week on..."
- "Eliminate the manual work of..."
- "Never deal with {{pain}} again"
- "Save {{X}} hours per week"
- "70% less time on..."

### Rules

- 3-4 sentences acceptable — more detail than ATL but still concise.
- Lead with daily pain. Name the specific frustration they deal with regularly.
- Quantify time / effort savings. "70% less time on reporting" beats "improved efficiency."
- Make them look good to their boss. BTL personas care about performance perception.

### Fatal BTL mistake

Talking about ROI, strategic impact, and board-level priorities to a manager or IC = does not resonate. Too abstract. They do not think in those terms.

### When to target BTL

- Product-led growth motion
- User adoption drives expansion
- Bottom-up sale
- Need an internal champion before reaching the budget holder

### BTL Examples

**Marketing Ops Manager**
```
Subject: 3 hours back

Hey {{firstName}},

Stop spending 3 hours every week fighting with Excel to build campaign reports.

We helped {{similar_company}}'s ops team cut their reporting time by 70%.

Want to see how?
```

**SDR Team Lead**
```
Subject: manual prospecting

Hey {{firstName}},

How much time does your team spend on manual list building each week?

Most SDR leads I talk to say 5-6 hours per rep.

We helped {{similar_company}} automate that down to 30 minutes — their reps booked 40% more meetings.

Worth a quick look?
```

**Data Analyst**
```
Subject: data cleanup

Hey {{firstName}},

If you are anything like other {{title}}s at {{industry}} companies, you are spending 10+ hours a week cleaning and merging data from different sources.

{{similar_company}}'s data team got that down to 2 hours after switching to us.

Want to see the workflow?
```

## Decision Framework Summary

| Target | When |
|--------|------|
| **ATL only** | Deal over $50K ACV. Transformation purchase. Top-down approval. |
| **BTL only** | Product-led growth. Bottom-up adoption. Internal champion needed. |
| **Both (multi-thread)** | Complex enterprise. Multiple stakeholders. Budget + user buy-in both needed. |

### Multi-thread sequencing

When threading both:
- BTL gets emailed first (build champion)
- ATL gets emailed 1-2 weeks later (after champion can reference user-side traction)
- Or simultaneously with different angles — never identical copy
