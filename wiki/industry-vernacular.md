# Industry Vernacular

Per-industry language guide. What to say, what to avoid, what signals "outsider" to a buyer in that vertical.

**Used by:** `gtm-skills/cold-email-writer.md` when writing for a sector-specific audience. `gtm-skills/client-onboarder.md` Phase 4 references this when capturing client voice.

**Updated as:** Harry takes on new verticals or learns from in-vertical replies.

---

## SaaS (B2B software)

### Use
- "Pipeline" (not "sales funnel" — outdated)
- "Stack" or "tech stack"
- "ICP" (everyone in SaaS knows this)
- "ARR" / "MRR" / "ACV"
- "Closed-won" / "closed-lost"
- "Time-to-value" (TTV)
- "PLG" — product-led growth
- "Self-serve" vs "sales-led"

### Avoid
- "Innovative solution" — meaningless
- "Best-in-class" — every vendor says this
- "Enterprise-grade" — unless company is genuinely enterprise
- "Synergy" — never
- "Marketing automation" — too generic; be specific (Marketo, Pardot, HubSpot, etc.)

### Insider tell
- Senior SaaS buyers love specific metrics ("47% lift" not "significant improvement")
- They respect concise asks
- They despise marketing fluff — every word earns its place

### Outsider tell
- Using "software" instead of "platform" or "product"
- Saying "users" when you mean "customers"
- Talking about features before outcomes

## FinTech

### Use
- "Rails" (payment rails, banking rails)
- "Compliance" — never an afterthought
- "KYC" / "AML" / "PCI"
- "Settlement" (for payments)
- "Underwriting"
- "Capital" (for lending)

### Avoid
- "Money movement" — say "payment flow" or "settlement"
- "Bank" if you mean "depository institution"
- "Crypto" outside of crypto-specific contexts (signals lack of seriousness in traditional fintech)

### Insider tell
- Compliance, regulation, and risk language gets attention
- Mentions of specific regulators (FinCEN, FCA, BaFin) signal seriousness

### Outsider tell
- Treating "fintech" as one homogenous market
- Conflating payments and lending
- Not acknowledging the compliance overhead

## HR / People Ops

### Use
- "People ops" or "talent" (newer)
- "Employee experience" (EX) — current term
- "Headcount"
- "Retention"
- "Workforce planning"
- "Performance review" (not "performance evaluation")
- "Ramp time" — for new hires

### Avoid
- "Human capital" — feels cold to People teams
- "HRIS" outside of admin contexts
- "Resource" when you mean "employee" (controversial in modern HR)

### Insider tell
- Mention of Lattice, BambooHR, Workday, Rippling, Gusto — these signal you know the tools
- Acknowledging the tension between People (function) and Finance (budget) gets nods

### Outsider tell
- Using "HR" exclusively (modern teams prefer "People")
- Treating retention as just about salary
- Ignoring DEI / culture context entirely

## Healthcare / Health Tech

### Use
- "Provider" (the institution) / "clinician" (the person)
- "Patient acquisition" (not "patient marketing")
- "Episode of care"
- "Population health"
- "PHI" — Protected Health Information
- "HIPAA"
- "ICD-10" / "CPT codes"
- "Payer" — the insurance side

### Avoid
- "Doctor" — say "physician" or "clinician"
- "Sales" when talking to clinical buyers (use "deployment" or "implementation")
- "Sick people" — uncomfortable; use "patient population"
- "Disrupting healthcare" — provokes (healthcare ≠ a market that needs disrupting)

### Insider tell
- HIPAA / SOC 2 / HITRUST mentions
- Acknowledgement of payer / provider / patient triangle
- Realism about long sales cycles

### Outsider tell
- Treating clinical and admin buyers identically
- Underestimating compliance complexity
- B2C marketing language ("delight your customers" doesn't work for hospitals)

## DevOps / Engineering Tools

### Use
- "Stack" (always)
- "Latency" / "throughput"
- "SRE" / "DevOps"
- "Observability" (not "monitoring" — different category)
- "Self-hosted" vs "managed"
- "Open source" / "OSS"
- "OpenTelemetry" (current standard)
- "Incident response"
- "On-call"

### Avoid
- "Solution" — engineers smell marketing
- "Easy" — nothing is easy
- "Best practices" — too consultative
- "Game-changer" — never

### Insider tell
- Specific tool mentions (Datadog, New Relic, Grafana, Prometheus, etc.)
- Acknowledging trade-offs ("X is fast but Y is more reliable")
- Saying "engineers" not "developers" (subtle but engineers prefer the former)

### Outsider tell
- Marketing language anywhere
- Generic claims about reliability or speed
- Not naming specific tools in the comparison space

## Recruitment / Staffing

### Use
- "Time-to-hire"
- "Cost-per-hire"
- "Quality-of-hire"
- "Candidate pipeline"
- "Source of hire"
- "Reqs" (requisitions)
- "Direct hire" vs "contract" vs "contract-to-hire"

### Avoid
- "Headhunter" — outdated, sometimes pejorative
- "Talent acquisition" outside of formal contexts (sounds HR-slang to hiring managers)
- "Closing" candidates — recruiters say "placing"

### Insider tell
- Mentions of Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby (current ATS market leaders)
- Understanding the diff between agency, RPO, and in-house recruiting
- Time-to-hire benchmarks by role type

### Outsider tell
- Conflating talent acquisition with HR
- Generic "we find great people" claims
- Not understanding success-fee vs retainer models

## E-commerce / DTC

### Use
- "DTC" (direct-to-consumer)
- "AOV" (average order value)
- "CAC" (customer acquisition cost)
- "LTV" (lifetime value)
- "CAC payback"
- "Repeat rate" / "repeat customer"
- "ROAS"
- "Channel mix" — paid social, email, organic, etc.

### Avoid
- "Brand" used vaguely — be specific (e.g. "performance brand")
- "Marketing funnel" — outdated
- "Customer journey" — corporate

### Insider tell
- Shopify Plus / WooCommerce / BigCommerce specific mentions
- Klaviyo (the email standard) / Postscript (SMS)
- Acknowledgement of post-iOS14 attribution challenges

### Outsider tell
- Generic "increase sales" claims
- Not differentiating between paid social and email economics
- Ignoring CAC payback realities

## Professional Services / Consulting

### Use
- "Engagement" (the project)
- "Statement of work" (SOW)
- "Practice" (the area of consulting)
- "Bench" (available consultants)
- "Utilisation"
- "Bill rate"

### Avoid
- "Customer" — say "client"
- "Salesperson" — say "partner" or "principal"
- "Pipeline" works but "opportunities" is more common

### Insider tell
- Acknowledgement of the talent-bench-utilisation pressure
- Understanding of fixed-fee vs T&M pricing
- Realism about long enterprise sales cycles

### Outsider tell
- Treating consulting like SaaS sales
- Generic "improve outcomes" claims
- Not understanding the partner / principal hierarchy

## How to Use This File

When writing copy for a new vertical:
1. Open this file
2. Find the closest vertical (or note it's missing — add it as you learn)
3. Use the "Use" terms in copy where natural
4. Strictly avoid the "Avoid" terms
5. If client falls between verticals (e.g. FinTech-HR tool) → blend both, prioritise the buyer's vertical

If you learn something new from a successful campaign:
- Add to "Insider tell" with the specific phrasing that landed
- Add to "Outsider tell" with the language that got pushback

This file gets richer over time. Treat it as a living vocabulary.
