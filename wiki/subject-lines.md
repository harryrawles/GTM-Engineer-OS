# Subject Lines

Subject lines decide whether the email gets opened. Preview text decides whether the open turns into a read. Together they are the cover of the book.

**The rule:** subject + preview = a complete thought. They work together. The first 50 characters of the email body appear in the inbox preview - write line 1 as if it is part of the subject line.

---

## Hard Rules

- **2-4 words.** Shorter outperforms in cold email.
- **All lowercase.** Feels casual and personal, not promotional.
- **No spam triggers.** Avoid "free", "guarantee", "limited time", "act now".
- **No punctuation overkill.** No exclamation marks. No ALL CAPS.
- **No question marks.** Question marks in subject lines signal "salesperson".
- **Relevant to the body.** Deceptive subject lines destroy trust and trigger spam filters.
- **Same thread for Email 2.** Email 2 uses `RE:` (same subject). Email 3 gets a new subject on a new thread.

## 4 Subject Line Formulas

### 1. Personalised (highest open rate)

Reference a specific data point about them.

```
{{trigger_topic}}
{{metric}} at {{company}}
{{number_of_years}} years
12 open roles
```

### 2. Curiosity Gap

Imply value without revealing it.

```
quick question
one thing about {{topic}}
{{company}} → {{outcome}}
```

### 3. Direct Value

State the outcome they would get.

```
3 hours back
$50k opportunity
Q1 revenue efficiency
{{number}} potential leads
```

### 4. Two-Word Formula (AI-generated)

Exactly 2 words. All lowercase. Relevant to their business. No sales / buzz words.

```
marketing data
hiring speed
pipeline math
vulnerability scan
revenue efficiency
```

**AI prompt for two-word generation:**
```
Write a two-word email subject line based on {{companyDomain}}.

Rules:
- MUST be exactly 2 words
- All lower case
- Relevant to user's business
- No sales or buzz words
- No spam triggers
- Should flow into the email naturally
```

## A/B Testing Subject Lines

- Test **2 subject lines at a time** (not more)
- Minimum **100 sends per variant** before judging
- Measure **open rate AND reply rate** - high opens with low replies = clickbait
- Test contrasting styles: personalised vs curiosity, short vs slightly longer
- Run **3 subject lines per campaign** in rotation if testing more broadly

## Subject + Preview Working Together

The preview text is the first ~50 characters of the email body. Always design them as a pair.

```
Subject: 12 open roles
Preview: Hey John, with {{company}} hiring 12 engineers, I assume ramp time is a priority...
```

```
Subject: tariffs
Preview: Hey John, with cost rising on imports from China, are you considering your options?
```

```
Subject: pipeline math
Preview: Hey John, if you booked 5 more demos a month at current close rate...
```

## What Kills Open Rate

- 7+ word subject lines
- Title Case ("Quick Question About Your Pipeline")
- Emojis (sometimes work in B2C, almost never in B2B cold)
- Brackets like [URGENT] or [Re:]
- Numbers that look like spam ("$$$", "100% guaranteed")
- Anything that screams "marketing email"

---

## Position Yourself as a Potential Customer in the Subject

A counter-intuitive ColdIQ insight: position yourself in the subject line as if you might be a potential customer. Their reps are conditioned to open emails from prospects.

```
Subject: pricing question
Subject: similar tool comparison
Subject: implementation timeline
```
