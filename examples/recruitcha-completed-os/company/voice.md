# Voice — Recruitcha (Example)

## Brand Voice

**Overall tone:** Direct, data-led, confident without arrogance.
**Personality:** Three words — sharp, knowledgeable, no-nonsense.
**What to avoid:** Corporate fluff. Marketing-speak. Vague claims. Overly casual ("hey buddy"). Aggressive ("act now").

## Writing Rules

- Use "I" not "we" in cold copy (single sender persona — Drew)
- Always cite specific numbers, never round up
- No jargon — write as if explaining to a smart non-recruiter
- Active voice. Short sentences.
- British or American spelling: **American** (US-only operations)

## Words to Use

| Preferred | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "Sourced from" | Direct, technical |
| "Apples to apples" | Drew's phrase, authentic |
| "Engineering velocity" | Resonates with technical buyers |
| "Vetted at intake" | Concrete process description |

## Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Replace with |
|-------|-------------|
| "Leverage" | "use" |
| "Synergy" | Remove entirely |
| "Innovative" | Specific claim instead |
| "World-class" | Specific proof point |
| "Best-in-class" | Specific proof point |
| "Game-changing" | Remove entirely |
| "Top talent" | "Senior engineers from VC-backed product companies" |
| "Talent acquisition" | "Hiring" or "engineering hires" |
| "Reach out" | "Send" or "drop a note" |

## Approved Claims

Claims explicitly approved by client for use in cold outreach:

- "Shortlist within 72 hours"
- "92% of roles filled within 21 days"
- "40-70% cost saving vs local US hires"
- "Saved TurboTenant $100k+ in hiring costs"
- "70+ funded startups placed with"
- "100+ engineer placements in 2025"
- "Success-only pricing — no hire, no fee"
- "90-day replacement guarantee"
- "Sourced exclusively from VC-backed product companies"

**Do not use claims not on this list without checking with Drew first.**

## Examples

### Good example (Drew's own writing)
> "Saw you're hiring 3 senior engineers. Most VPs of Eng I talk to are losing 10-15 hrs/week interviewing candidates from outsourcing firms. We only source from VC-backed product companies — TurboTenant's VP closed 4 hires this way last quarter, saved $100k. Worth a sample shortlist?"

### Bad example
> "We are excited to introduce our innovative talent acquisition solution. Our best-in-class platform leverages cutting-edge AI to source top talent for forward-thinking organizations."

Why bad: corporate fluff, every word is generic, no proof, no specificity, no human voice.

## Industry Vernacular Notes

- VC-backed startups use "engineering" not "tech" or "development team"
- Refer to roles by seniority + function: "senior fullstack" not "developer"
- "Fund" is fine — these are all funded companies
- Avoid "talent" except in formal contexts ("talent acquisition" sounds like HR slang to engineering buyers)
- Drew uses "rep" / "AE" / "BDR" — these are SaaS-native and OK to use back

## Voice Notes

- Drew is American (Texas-based). Use US English throughout. ("color" not "colour", "optimize" not "optimise", "z" not "s" in -ize / -ise endings.)
- No emojis in cold copy.
- Signature should be just "Harry" — first name only, no title.
- Drew is comfortable with casual phrasing in DMs but expects emails to be tight and direct.
