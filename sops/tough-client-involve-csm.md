# SOP: Tough Client, Involve CSM

Who: GTMEs.

When: a client is becoming difficult, disengaged, or consuming more time than is reasonable.

Your job is delivery: campaigns, lists, optimisation. When a client situation starts eating into that,
flag it. Do not absorb it alone.

---

## Flag It to CSM Early

If a client is pushing back, losing confidence, or becoming hard work, tell the CSM. Don't wait for it to
escalate. A quick flag early is far easier to handle than a churn situation later.

Give CSM the context in one message:
- Client name and channel
- What the client has said or is doing
- What you've already tried
- What you need: space to keep building, a client reset, a strategic conversation

---

## What CSM Will Do

CSM steps in to provide air cover, not to take over, but to reset the dynamic and buy you the space to
keep building.

- Client losing confidence before results have had time to land: CSM reframes expectations and explains
  the testing framework.
- Client pushing on results week after week: CSM takes ownership of that conversation so you're not
  fighting the same battle every week.
- Client relationship deteriorating: CSM takes the relationship layer so you can focus on delivery.
- Client needs a more senior or strategic voice: CSM provides that without undermining you. If the
  confidence issue also touches billing, contract, or scope, flag AM in parallel per
  `sops/gtme-escalation-to-am.md`, this is a dual-flag, not a choice between the two. AM works the
  commercial lever, CSM works the relationship lever.

The goal is to keep you in your lane. Use CSM as the buffer when client friction starts pulling you away
from campaign work.

## Where this fits in the OS

- **This generalises the single situation already named in `sops/client-communication-principles.md`**
  ("Client wants to cancel", flag to the CSM same day). That row is the acute case, a client already at
  the point of cancelling. This SOP is the earlier, broader trigger: flag CSM on the friction well before
  it becomes a cancellation conversation.
- **This is a different axis to `sops/am-gtme-responsibility-split.md`.** That document is about who
  executes a task (AM for scope, billing, technical fixes). This SOP is about who holds the relationship
  when it's under strain. A client demanding work outside scope is an AM scope-reminder; a client losing
  confidence or burning disproportionate time is a CSM flag, the two can both apply to the same client at
  once.
- **`gtm-skills/client-health-scorer.md`'s Red and Critical bands** now point here: a health score drop
  driven by relationship deterioration, not just PRR, is this SOP's trigger, don't wait for the score to
  bottom out before flagging.
- **This is not `sops/gtme-unlock-ladder.md`.** The Unlock Ladder is for when GTME is stuck on solving a
  campaign. This SOP is for when the client relationship itself, not the campaign, is the problem. The two
  can run in parallel on the same account.
- **CSM's own SOPs (cancellation-save, escalation routing) live outside this repo entirely.** This SOP only
  documents GTME's side of the interface, what to flag and when, not CSM's process once flagged.
- **`sops/gtme-escalation-to-am.md` is the equivalent document for AM.** The two aren't mutually exclusive,
  see its "senior voice" trigger for the dual-flag case.
- **`sops/churn-prevention.md` names this SOP as its "Early Signal, At-Risk Intervention" step.** Same
  trigger set, dropping reply rate, negative tone, missed responses. That SOP also covers onboarding-time
  prevention and the cancellation-save discount boundary, both outside this SOP's scope.
