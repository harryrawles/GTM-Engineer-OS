# SOP: Client Not Getting Results

Who: GTMEs.

When: results are below expectation at any point in the engagement, or campaigns aren't performing and
you've exhausted your own angles.

This must be addressed early, not when it becomes a problem. Do not wait. Do not sit on it.

At all times, you must be able to clearly answer two questions:
- Here is what we are currently doing, and where we are with it.
- Here is what we are changing or testing to improve it.

If you cannot answer both clearly, you are not ready to speak to the client.

---

## Step 1 - Get In Front of It

The moment you see results underperforming, act. Do not wait for the client to raise it. You raise it
first. Getting ahead of it is the entire point.

---

## Step 2 - Know Your Position Before Every Client Conversation

Before speaking to the client, be clear on:
- What is currently running and where we are with it.
- What is being changed or tested to improve it.

Do not communicate a problem without a plan attached to it.

---

## Step 3 - Work Through the Unlock Ladder

If you have hit a wall, do not sit on it. Follow `sops/gtme-unlock-ladder.md` in order, most problems get
solved before you ever reach Step 4:
1. Query Cowork.
2. Post in your support channel with a Loom.
3. Post in #grill-my-campaigns.

These three resources are your toolkit, use them all before anything else.

---

## Step 4 - Own It Completely

- You own the results.
- You own the communication.
- You own the plan to fix it.

Do not wait to be asked. Do not let the client feel like they are chasing you. Do not communicate a
problem without a plan attached.

---

## Where this fits in the OS

- **This is the proactive half of `sops/client-communication-principles.md`'s "Be Proactive" principle.**
  That principle states the standard ("deal with errors before the client notices them"); this SOP is the
  full procedure for the specific, highest-stakes case of it: results underperforming.
- **Step 3 is `sops/gtme-unlock-ladder.md`, run in full.** This SOP is one of the situations that ladder
  exists for, diagnosis-and-fix, not ownership. A campaign that's stuck for an ownership reason (technical
  fault, domain issue) is `sops/am-gtme-responsibility-split.md`'s "Campaign not performing" row instead.
- **`gtm-skills/client-request-handler.md` Type I ("Performance concern")** is what happens when Step 1 of
  this SOP was missed and the client raised it first. The goal is for Type I to never fire because the
  client already knows and already has a plan in hand.
- **`gtm-skills/client-report-writer.md`'s Red-campaign rule and `gtm-skills/qbr-writer.md`'s Underperformer
  line** ("name it and the fix, don't dodge") are Step 2 of this SOP, applied to the weekly report and the
  quarterly review respectively. Neither should ever surface a red campaign the client is hearing about for
  the first time.
- **Diagnosis itself runs through `gtm-skills/campaign-optimiser.md`.** This SOP governs what happens
  around that diagnosis, when you disclose it, what you say, and what you do if the diagnosis stalls, not
  the diagnostic method itself.
